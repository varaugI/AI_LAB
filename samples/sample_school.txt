INTRODUCTION TO PHOTOSYNTHESIS

Learning objective: explain how plants convert light energy into chemical energy.
Photosynthesis occurs mainly in chloroplasts. Chlorophyll absorbs light. During the light-dependent reactions, water contributes electrons and oxygen is released. The Calvin cycle uses carbon dioxide to build sugars. The simplified formula is carbon dioxide plus water plus light energy producing glucose and oxygen.

Exercise: Why is chlorophyll important? Answer: chlorophyll absorbs the light energy that begins the process.
